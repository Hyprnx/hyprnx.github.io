# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curious visitors arriving from LinkedIn, Instagram bios, dating app profiles, or direct links. They want to understand not just a name, but a person: career trajectory, personal philosophy, creative taste. The first impression should land as "bold, sci-fi-literate technologist who builds real things." They're scanning quickly, but the design should reward those who linger.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for To Duc Anh (Hyprnx), a Senior Data Engineer based in Hanoi. The site is the brand itself: it communicates technical depth, aesthetic ambition, and a point of view. It should feel like stepping into someone's world, not reading a resume. Success looks like a visitor remembering the experience and wanting to know more.

## Brand Personality

Cutting-edge. Future. Reliable.

Voice is confident but never loud. Speaks in specifics, not buzzwords. The tone sits at the intersection of a research paper's precision and a sci-fi novel's atmosphere. There's warmth underneath the technical surface: someone who builds serious infrastructure but also has a sense of play.

Emotional goals: technical credibility, quiet confidence, bold thinking, futuristic wonder.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated portfolio templates (Claude, Codex, v0 defaults). If it looks like someone typed "make me a portfolio" into a chatbot, it's failed.
- Static backgrounds with no life or movement. The site should breathe.
- Bootstrap/template-kit resume layouts. The initial version of this site was exactly that. We're done with it.
- Overly flashy particle effects for their own sake (the second version's particle.js was decoration without purpose).
- Corporate SaaS landing pages with hero-metric grids and identical feature cards.
- "Developer portfolio" dark mode with neon green on black. That's the training-data reflex for this category.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the world, don't describe it.** The site should feel like an environment you enter, not a document you read. Atmosphere over information architecture.
2. **Bold restraint.** Make strong choices, then hold back just enough to create tension. Mysterious means leaving space for the visitor to lean in.
3. **Earned complexity.** Every visual layer (motion, depth, glow) must serve the narrative. Nothing decorative without purpose.
4. **Fiction-grade craft.** The reference point is science fiction interfaces: not the flashy ones, but the ones that feel inevitable. Credible futures, not fantasy dashboards.
5. **Reward curiosity.** The site should have depth that reveals itself to people who explore. First glance is bold; second look is rich; third look is surprising.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. Ensure sufficient contrast ratios on all text, especially over translucent or gradient surfaces. No information conveyed by color alone.
